Checkout Redesign Case Study

A deep dive into the role I played improving user experience and increasing conversion rates

Overview

The checkout redesign was initiated to address a declining conversion rate and poor user experience in the final steps of the purchasing process. Our goal was to streamline the checkout process, reduce friction, and ultimately increase conversions.

Reasearch

Common friction points solutions pitched and new layouts presented

We conducted user interviews, analyzed heatmaps, and reviewed customer feedback to understand pain points. The data showed that users were dropping off at specific steps in the checkout process.

Based on our research, we created low-fidelity wireframes to simplify the checkout process. After several rounds of iteration, we developed high-fidelity prototypes for user testing.

The Solution

We implemented a streamlined checkout process with fewer steps, clearer input fields, and a mobile-first design. The new design reduced cognitive load by minimizing distractions and highlighting key actions.

Poor Responsiveness

Users encountered repeated errors during checkout without clear instructions on how to resolve them. This led to frustration and ultimately caused many users to abandon their carts before completing their purchase.

Outcome

Metrics

  • 15% increase in conversion rates
  • 25% reduction in cart abandonment
  • 30% increase in mobile checkout completions
